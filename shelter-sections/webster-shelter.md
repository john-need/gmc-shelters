Mile 3.0 — Stratton Mt Tr. — Division 3

This shelter was a frame building with bunks for 7. It was built by the Worcester section in 1926. A stream 600 ft to the west provided water. It was in use from 1926 to the fall of 1988 when rt was abandoned due to trail reloca1ion. [GB 17th Edition 1963] No longer on the LT and in poo- condition. [GB 12th Edition 1940] The old Shelter is still maintained as a regular LT Shelter. Bunk space for 6. [1955 Supplement to the 1951 LT GB) I got J. W. Allen, a carpenter from W. Wardsboro to repair Webster. He put on new roofing where needed, new roof boards on the overhang front, new support posts in front. boards on the bunks, built a new table, and cleaned out a fireplace someone had built inside the camp. As you know it was our plan to abandon that camp as soon as we could build at Grout Pond and open the new trail, but we must keep Webster up until then. Cost us $40. to repair it but it is in good shape again now. (LT News, Nov. 1945]

In late April 1969 Allan Mead and party went into the shelter near the West-Wardsboro/East-Arlington Road and did a masterful job of rejuvenating this shelter. They ascertained the condition of the shelter, listed what they needed, phoned some other Connecticut enthusiasts, who brought up the material, and in this one trip refurbished the shelter including a new roof. [LT News. August. 1969]
