About 1856 the Underhill land records show a conveyance of 52.5 acres of land from John B. Wheeler and Mary C. R. Wheeler to Francis Cahill. This location is near the present junction of the CCC Road and the Halfway House Trail. With the sale of land came the exclusive right and control to the mountain trail to a point near the Summit House on the main ridge of the mountain. The Halfway House surely was used by many mountain trampers. GB 1st Edition 1917 refers to ruins of this hotel. Shown as a closed shelter on GM map 10th Edition 1935. This site can be used by hikers but is not maintained by the GMC. [GB 14th Edition 1951]
