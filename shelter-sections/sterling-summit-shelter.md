On Sterling summit is a frame of shelter built in 1915, which can be thatched with fir boughs to afford rude shelter to 3 or 4 people. [GB 1st Edition 1917] During the summer of 1915, the local paper reported that members of the Sterling Section had built a shelter Lodge with root boughs at the summit of Sterling and travelers can now safely plan to remain there overnight. [Sterling Section History by Robert L Hagerman]
