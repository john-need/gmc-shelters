In 1899 Colonel Joseph Battell built a buckboard road to the summit of Mt. Abraham and a trail along the skyline of Lincoln Mountain. He built a lodge below the summit of Abraham where he entertained many guests. He built another lodge on the northern end of the mountain four years later. Although his ridge line trail predated the Long Trail. It is probably the same trail that exists today along much of the Lincoln Range and one of the earliest sections of the present-day LT. Col. Battell's hotels probably were some of the first LT overnight accommodations. This hotel was located 100 ft left on an old road from Mile 6.7 of Division 8 in the GB 23rd Edition 1985.
