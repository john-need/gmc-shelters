This shelter was constructed in 1975 by the Burlington Section's "Rat Patrol," with the assistance of shelter replaces a log cabin which burned in 1974 and is named in memory of Louis B. Puffer, long active in many capacities with the GMC. It has bunk space fa- 6 to 8. A small stream to the west is an uncertain source of water in cry seasons. [GB 23rd Edition 1985] A tremendous response to an appeal by the Burlington Section to rebuild the Lodge resulted in its completion in about 24 hours. The big problem was carrying the precut materials 3 miles from the road head to the site. 125 people showed up at the Bolton Valley Corp. parking lot at 8 a.m. on June 14, 1975. Undeterred by flies, mud, humidity, and threat of thunderstorms, the volunteers carried everything to the site. By 2 p.m. the next day the job was done. [LTN, August 1975]
